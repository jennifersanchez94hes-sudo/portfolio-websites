Results Summary

I passed the Google Analytics 4 Certification exam with a score of 100%. The exam covered a lot of different GA4 topics, and it made me understand how the setup, reports, events, and analysis tools connect to each other. It also helped me see that GA4 is not just about checking numbers, but about using data to make better marketing decisions.

Top 3 Most Challenging Topics

  • Understanding attribution models and knowing when each one makes sense.
  • Figuring out the difference between standard reports, explorations, and advertising reports.
  • Remembering the different GA4 settings, like data retention, integrations, and measurement features.

Two Questions I Wish I Could Redo

One concept I wish I could redo was attribution because I had to slow down and think about which model fit the situation best. Another area I would want to redo was explorations, especially choosing the right type of exploration for a specific analysis goal. I understood the general idea, but I think more practice would help me feel more confident.

CEP Connection

One thing I will change in my CEP measurement and analysis plan is adding a Funnel Exploration instead of only using basic acquisition reports. This is more useful because it can show where users drop off before completing an important action, such as a purchase, signup, or lead form. I will also compare traffic sources so I can see which channels bring in users who are actually engaged, not just which channels bring the most visits.
